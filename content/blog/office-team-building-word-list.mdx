## Office Party Game Strategies

### For New Teams

**Focus on**:
- Universal office experiences (meetings, coffee)
- Avoid inside jokes
- Use equipment everyone sees daily
- Keep it light and positive

### For Established Teams

**Include**:
- Company-specific terminology
- Inside jokes about your office
- Specific software your team uses
- Recent company events

### For Mixed Seniority

**Balance**:
- Include both old and new tech (landline vs Slack)
- Mix traditional and modern work terms
- Allow for generational differences
- Avoid words only executives would know

## Ice Breaker Variations

### New Hire Orientation Game

**How it works**:
1. Use only office-specific vocabulary
2. New hires learn terminology while playing
3. Veterans explain context
4. Makes onboarding memorable

**Sample words**:
- Your company's software names
- Office locations/rooms
- Internal systems
- Department names

### Department Mixer

**Concept**:
Mix words from different departments:
- Finance uses marketing terms
- IT describes HR processes
- Sales explains operations vocabulary

**Result**: Cross-departmental understanding!

### Remote Team Building

**Virtual-friendly approach**:
- Use screen-sharing to show words
- Breakout rooms for small groups
- Chat feature for guessing
- Perfect for distributed teams

A: Check with management. Frame it as team-building professional development.

**Q: What about remote teams?**

**Q: How do we handle competitive coworkers?**
